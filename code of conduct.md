# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

Everything Open Source is a non-profit community dedicated to making open-source contributions accessible to all across Africa and beyond

---

## Who This Applies To

This Code of Conduct applies to all community members, including:
- Contributors to any Everything Open Source project or repository
- Attendees and participants of sprints, events, and meetups
- Members of our online spaces, including GitHub, social media, and community forums
- Speakers, facilitators, and organizers representing the community

It applies in all community spaces and in any one-on-one communications that take place in the context of community activities.

---

## Our Values

### Openness
We welcome contributions from people of all skill levels and backgrounds. Open source is for everyone, and we actively create space for newcomers, 
including those making their very first contribution.

### Respect
We treat every community member with dignity. Differences in opinion, approach, and experience are expected and valued. 
Disagreement is handled with kindness and professionalism.

### Collaboration
We build together. We share knowledge, support each other, and celebrate contributions across code, documentation, 
design, research, and community support.

### Accessibility
We are committed to lowering barriers to participation. We work to make our resources, events, and tools usable and understandable 
by as many people as possible.

### Integrity
We are honest, transparent, and accountable. We acknowledge mistakes, learn from them, and work to improve continuously.

---

## Expected Behavior

All community members are expected to:

- Be welcoming, kind, and patient with others, especially those who are new
- Use inclusive language and be mindful of how your words may affect others
- Respect differing viewpoints and experiences
- Accept constructive feedback gracefully and offer it thoughtfully
- Give credit to others for their ideas and contributions
- Be honest about your own experience level and limitations
- Focus on what is best for the community, not just your individual goals
- Show up prepared and engaged at events and sprints
- Respect the time and effort of organizers, maintainers, and fellow contributors

---

## Unacceptable Behavior

The following behaviors are not tolerated in the Everything Open Source community:

- Harassment, intimidation, or discrimination of any kind
- Use of sexualized language, imagery, or unwelcome advances
- Trolling, personal attacks, or deliberately inflammatory comments
- Public or private insults directed at any community member
- Sharing someone's personal information without their consent (doxxing)
- Dismissing or belittling contributions based on a person's experience level or background
- Deliberately disrupting conversations, events, or collaborative work
- Plagiarism or misrepresentation of another person's work
- Any conduct that could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting

---

## Reporting an Issue

If you witness or experience behavior that violates this Code of Conduct, we encourage you to report it. You can do so by:

- Reaching out directly to a community organizer or sprint co-lead at any event
- Submitting a written report via our email at everythingopensource@gmail.com
- Sending a private message to a trusted community leader

All reports will be treated with confidentiality and seriousness. You will not be penalized for reporting in good faith.

## Contribution Guidelines

Everything Open Source welcomes contributions across many areas:

- **Code** — bug fixes, features, and improvements to our repositories
- **Documentation** — improving clarity, fixing errors, and adding missing content
- **Design** — visual assets, interface improvements, and accessibility enhancements
- **Community support** — answering questions, mentoring new contributors, and facilitating discussions
- **Research and writing** — blog posts, case studies, and community resources

When contributing, please follow any specific guidelines provided in individual project repositories. 
When in doubt, ask. We would rather answer a question than see someone held back by uncertainty.

---

## Acknowledgment

By participating in the Everything Open Source community, you agree to uphold this Code of Conduct. 
We are grateful for every person who takes the time to contribute to a more open and accessible open-source world.

This Code of Conduct is inspired by the [Contributor Covenant](https://www.contributor-covenant.org) and 
adapted to reflect the values and mission of Everything Open Source.
